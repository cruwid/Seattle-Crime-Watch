Research

For our research, we referred 4 websites to gather various aspects of the visualizations. Firstly we referred Lee Byron's Heat maps where he calculates the walk scores for various places to decide how easy it is to live without a car [1]. We can use the heat map for the intensity of the crimes in the city of Seattle. Next, to get more information on our idea about analyzing crime according to the time of the day, we referred the article by Trulia Trends [2], where they measured the crime rates and used graph to show a graphic of the same. They also had a graphic to show the crime rates in Seattle where using maps with hues, they showed the crime rates in different areas of Seattle [3]. The Trulia trends team could get some really insightful results about type and time of crime. Lastly, to visualize data over space and time we referred the graphic by Kevin Bell which analyses the crime in Salt Lake city [4].
